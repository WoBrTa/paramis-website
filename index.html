01 The Shift

The future knowledge worker is an agent manager.

They will not type into AI tools. They will direct AI agents to perform work, verify the output, iterate, and ship. This is fundamentally a management skill, not a technical skill. It requires the ability to delegate clearly, set quality standards, review work critically, and iterate based on gaps. This shift is not a prediction. It is underway.

02 The Gap

Most organizations are not preparing their people for this.

They are running pilots without plans, training without structure, and adopting without governance. The gap between what AI can do and what their workforce is ready for grows wider every quarter. Readiness is not a product you buy. It is a capability you build.

03 The Through-Line

The same person who presents the roadmap also builds the first prototype.

Readiness requires continuity from strategy to desktop. The three-year AI transformation plan, the governance framework, the nine-week training program, and the first working automation. One engagement. One person. No hand-offs, no translation loss, no strategy that never made it to the work floor.

Services

Advisory and Deployment

Plan

Design your AI Journey

AI changes fast. I map that change across six dimensions of your business: productivity, governance, data, products, customers, and vendors. I shape a path for upskilling, safer tech adoption, and realizing benefits as you're ready. The result is a phased, multi-year roadmap to drive your decisioning and build your future capabilities.

Train

Build your Agent Managers

I teach your employees to become Agent Managers, through my Foundations and Advanced programs. I take knowledge workers from first interaction to delegation fluency: the ability to direct AI, verify its output, and iterate until the work is done. I also embed with teams to co-design automations, run design sprints, and help you evaluate adoption.

Deploy

Affect change at your business

You're not deploying a tool. Deploying AI assistants and agents is a change management event. Static work becomes a continual learning path, and safer adoption practices create new governance needs. I build governance frameworks tailored to your industry and develop the policies you need to navigate change with your workforce, your customers, and your strategies.

Solution

Build tools for your business

I design and build AI solutions for specific business problems: multi-agent workflows, automated evaluation systems, and purpose-built tools inside platforms like ChatGPT and Claude. For larger enterprises with complex infrastructure needs, I help evaluate vendors, shape solution architecture, and coordinate deployment across teams. Every solution is keyed to a discovered need and quantifiable value.

Inform

Public speaking and group events

AI is moving faster than most people can track. I stay on top of it so your audience doesn't have to. I speak to industry associations, community organizations, trade groups, and corporate teams about what is changing and what they can do about it. Conference keynotes, executive briefings, and general presentations. Tell me what you need.

Educate

Teaching everyone how to use AI

I run hands-on AI workshops for adults who want to make this technology work in their own lives. These can be multi-week programs, hackathons, or workshops built on the same concepts I deliver to corporate teams, adapted for individual learners. Participants learn to use AI safely, evaluate what they see, and build habits that will matter.

About

Woody Taylor

I help companies prepare their people and operations for a future where every knowledge worker manages AI agents as part of their daily job. That shift is already underway, and most organizations are not ready for it. My work is building that readiness: the strategic plans, the governance, the training programs, and the applied automations that turn AI from an experiment into how work gets done.

Through Paramis, I work at every level. I advise leadership on AI transformation roadmaps, design governance policies for regulated environments, train teams through structured multi-week programs, and sit at desks to co-build automations. The same person who presents the three-year plan also builds the first working prototype. That continuity is what makes adoption stick.

I bring two decades of leading operational and technology transformations in healthcare, banking, and enterprise services. That background in Six Sigma, change management, and enterprise transformation means I treat AI adoption the way it should be treated: as a real transformation program with phased milestones, measurable outcomes, and governance that holds up under scrutiny.

My approach is pragmatic and workforce-centered: equip people with real skills, embed the habit of verifying AI output, and give leaders the governance and metrics to steer adoption with confidence.
