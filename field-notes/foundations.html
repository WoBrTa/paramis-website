The Problem Everyone Recognizes

Most organizations approach AI training the same way: book a half-day workshop, show people how to write prompts, hand out a PDF, and hope for the best. Six weeks later, adoption has flatlined. The people who were already curious kept experimenting. Everyone else went back to how they worked before.

The training did not fail because the content was bad. It failed because it was designed to be an event, not a system. A single session cannot change how an organization works. It can only create a moment of enthusiasm that fades as soon as the next quarterly deadline hits.

"You would not teach someone to manage a team in a half-day workshop. AI capability is the same kind of skill."

AI as a Work System

Foundations starts from a different premise. ChatGPT and Claude are not productivity hacks you demonstrate in an afternoon. They are a system of intelligences, modalities, and tools that changes how knowledge work gets done. Teaching people to use that system takes weeks, not hours. It takes practice, not slides. And it takes a curriculum designed for how adults actually learn in a workplace: some people read, some watch, some listen, some need to try it with their hands on the keyboard.

At Paramis, I provide a transformation experience you can scale. Foundations is not a lecture series about AI. It is a structured, multi-week program where your people build real skills with real tools in the context of their actual jobs. Every module teaches a specific capability. Every capability maps to how your team works today and how they could work tomorrow.

What Nine Weeks Looks Like

Foundations is an eleven-module curriculum delivered over nine weeks. Each week, your people work through a module together. There is a live session, but the session is not the training. The training is the full content kit that ships with every module: eight distinct formats designed for different learning styles, different schedules, and different levels of engagement.

Presentations and data files for the live instruction. Full session recordings for people who missed the meeting or want to review. Short skill videos that demonstrate one technique in under five minutes, no editing, no production overhead, just a live demonstration in ChatGPT. Audio summaries for the commute. Visual summaries for a quick refresh. Infographics that people pin to their desk or screenshot into Slack. Function-specific prompt packs with ready-to-run Deep Research prompts tailored to the learner's actual domain. And custom GPTs, led by the Knowledge Bot, that stay deployed as permanent co-facilitators between sessions and long after the engagement ends.

The result is that by week three, your people are not watching someone else use AI. They are using it in their work, building on what they learned last week, and getting better at it each session.

"Most AI training is a webinar and a PDF. We ship a learning system."

Why Multi-Format Matters

The point is not volume. Volume is easy to produce and easy to ignore. The point is persistence and accessibility. Different people learn differently. Some read. Some watch. Some listen. Some interact. Some need a quick reference on their desk. Some need a prompt they can run in their next meeting.

By shipping all eight formats for every module, the training embeds itself into the organization's daily workflow rather than living in a forgotten SharePoint folder. The recordings get watched. The infographics get pinned. The prompt packs get used. And the Knowledge Bot keeps answering questions in real time, in the learner's own context, for as long as the organization keeps it deployed.

What Stays Behind

This is the part that matters most to the people writing the check. When the engagement ends, the organization does not go back to zero. The content kit stays. The recordings stay. The prompt packs stay. The Knowledge Bot stays. The skills your people built over nine weeks are reinforced by a system that keeps working after the consultant is gone.

That is the design. Not a training event that creates a burst of enthusiasm. A training product that outlasts the engagement and becomes part of how the organization operates. The consultant leaves. The learning system does not.

"If your people can only learn from a live session, you have a training problem, not an AI problem."
